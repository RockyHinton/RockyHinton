## Rocky Hinton

Final-year Computer Science student at Newcastle University, on track for a First-Class degree, with a focus on distributed systems, backend engineering, and real-world applications of decentralised technology.

My work sits at the intersection of traditional system design and emerging distributed infrastructure. I’m particularly interested in how coordination, trust, and settlement evolve as systems become more autonomous and machine-driven.

## Focus

I am currently focused on distributed ledger technology as a foundational layer for:

real-world asset tokenisation
machine-to-machine economic interaction
verifiable data and compute
trust infrastructure in increasingly autonomous systems

I see DLT not as a purely financial innovation, but as a coordination layer for systems that require shared state, verifiability, and trust without central dependency.

I aim to build systems that go beyond isolated features and instead reflect real-world use cases.

This includes:

- full-stack platforms with structured data relationships and user workflows
- blockchain implementations and smart contract systems
- simulation environments for evaluating distributed architectures
- prototypes that explore how decentralised infrastructure integrates with existing systems

## Academic foundation

My academic work has focused heavily on system-level thinking and performance:

- distributed systems
- database design and data modelling
- backend architecture and API design
- DevOps and system deployment
- concurrency and system behaviour under load

Alongside this, my dissertation explores the role of distributed ledger technology as a coordination and settlement layer for autonomous EV charging in smart-city environments, comparing decentralised and centralised architectures under controlled conditions.

## Direction

I am working towards building and contributing to systems that operate at scale, particularly in areas where:

- trust cannot be assumed
- coordination is distributed
- systems interact autonomously

This includes infrastructure around tokenisation, oracle networks, and verifiable compute.
